Why This Exists

When production systems fail, engineers don’t get clear answers—they get logs.

Thousands of lines, mixed signals, and hidden errors make it difficult to identify what actually went wrong. During critical incidents such as payment failures or server crashes, developers are forced to search through large volumes of noisy data just to find a single root cause.

This process is slow, stressful, error-prone, and costly in terms of downtime.

The Real Problem

The challenge is not a lack of data—it is an excess of unstructured data.

Logs are:

  • Noisy, filled with irrelevant information
  • Disconnected, with errors appearing without clear relationships
  • Difficult to interpret, especially under time pressure

Engineers do not need more logs. They need clarity, prioritization, and actionable insight.

What RBR Does

RBR transforms raw logs into clear, structured decisions. Instead of manual analysis, it:

  • Identifies the true root cause
  • Filters out irrelevant noise
  • Prioritizes critical issues
  • Explains what went wrong
  • Generates precise steps to resolve the problem

Built for Real Engineering Workflows

RBR is designed not just to analyze, but to support real operational workflows. It:

  • Proposes executable remediation scripts
  • Provides transparent reasoning for each decision
  • Requires user approval before any action is taken

This ensures both speed and control in high-stakes environments.

Impact

With RBR:

  • Debugging time is significantly reduced
  • Incident resolution becomes faster and more reliable
  • System downtime is minimized
  • Engineers can focus on solving problems rather than searching for them

RBR converts complex, unstructured logs into clear, actionable decisions—enabling faster and more reliable incident response.
